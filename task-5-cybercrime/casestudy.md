# Case Study: UPI Fraud - The Fake PG Scam

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud occurs when cybercriminals deceive victims into authorizing unauthorized money transfers. Rather than using complex hacking techniques to breach the banking system, scammers rely heavily on social engineering to manipulate human psychology and urgency.

A highly prevalent example across Indian university towns is the "Fake PG Accommodation" scam. Here is a step-by-step breakdown of how this cybercrime typically unfolds: First, a student desperately searching for housing near their college campus spots a visually appealing, budget-friendly room listed on a platform like Facebook Groups or OLX. The student contacts the supposed "landlord," who immediately creates a false sense of extreme urgency by claiming that several other students are waiting to finalize the booking.

To secure the room, the scammer demands a quick ₹5,000 token advance and sends a payment link via WhatsApp. Crucially, they assure the student that this link will simply "generate a digital receipt" or "verify their bank identity." When the anxious student clicks the link, their UPI application opens and prompts them for their secure PIN. Believing they are merely confirming their details to hold the room, the student enters the PIN. Instantly, the funds are deducted, the scammer blocks their contact number, and the fraudulent listing vanishes completely.

These criminals systematically target first-year college students and young professionals who are stressed, inexperienced, and rushing to secure housing. The consequences are severe. Beyond the immediate, unrecoverable financial loss—which is devastating on a limited student budget—victims often suffer from extreme anxiety, emotional distress, and a lasting loss of trust in digital payment ecosystems.

*(Source: Scenario based on UPI fraud advisories from the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal)*
