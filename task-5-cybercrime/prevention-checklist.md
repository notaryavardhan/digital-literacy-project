# 🛡️ Stay Safe Online: The Ultimate College Survival Checklist

Navigating the digital world as a college student can be tricky. Here are 8 real-world tips to keep your data and your wallet safe:

### 💸 UPI & Financial Safety
1. **The "Receiving Money Doesn't Need a PIN" Rule:** Seriously, if someone is sending *you* money for that old textbook you sold online or splitting a dinner bill, you will never, ever need to enter your UPI PIN. Your PIN is strictly for when money is leaving your account.
2. **Double-Check the Actual Name:** Before you hit "Pay" at the campus canteen or to a random auto driver, take two seconds to read the registered name that pops up on your screen. If you're paying your friend "Rahul" but the UPI name says "Xyz Enterprises," pause and ask questions before confirming.

### 📱 Everyday Digital Habits
3. **The "Fake Urgency" Red Flag:** Scammers absolutely love to make you panic. If someone calls you claiming your SIM card will be blocked in 10 minutes, or a landlord says "pay right this second or lose the room," take a deep breath and hang up. Real authorities don't force you to make split-second financial decisions.
4. **Campus Wi-Fi is for Studying, Not Banking:** I know the free college Wi-Fi is super tempting to use all day, but open networks are basically a playground for hackers. Never log into your bank account, pay your semester fees, or type in sensitive passwords while connected to public campus Wi-Fi. Switch to your mobile data first!
5. **Stop Reusing Your ERP Password:** We all have that one password we use for literally everything, but please don't use your college login password for your personal Gmail or banking apps. If a random portal gets breached, you don't want someone having the keys to your entire digital life. 
6. **Turn on 2FA (Two-Factor Authentication):** Yes, it's a tiny bit annoying to wait for an OTP every time you log in on a new device, but it is a lifesaver. Turn on 2FA for your Instagram, WhatsApp, and Gmail. Even if a hacker guesses your password, they can't get in without holding your actual phone.
7. **Treat Unknown Links Like Spam:** If you get a random SMS or a forwarded WhatsApp message saying you won a free laptop, or that your package delivery failed (even if you didn't order anything!), do not click the link. It's almost always a phishing attempt trying to steal your data or install malware.
8. **Lock Down Your Social Media:** As students, we love sharing our lives, but keep your profiles private. Don't post pictures that clearly show your college ID card barcode, your exact hostel room number, or real-time location updates. Scammers and stalkers can easily piece those details together.

### 🚨 Emergency Reporting Channels
If you or a friend ever become a victim of cybercrime or financial fraud, don't panic and don't feel embarrassed—report it immediately:
* **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
* **National Cybercrime Helpline:** Dial **1930** (Available 24x7)
